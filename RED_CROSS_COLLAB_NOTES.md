# be lahza — Red Cross collaboration readiness notes

## Why this version is safer and more serious

The app separates real emergencies from practice mode:

- Real emergency screens prioritize calling, SOS location, and simple action.
- AI Coach is labelled as training/practice only.
- The app never asks users to use AI before calling help.
- Animated guides are short and repeatable.
- Certified videos can be added later without redesigning the UX.

## AI Coach validation plan

For collaboration with first-aid instructors, the AI Coach should be validated through:

1. Instructor-approved checklist for CPR posture:
   - full upper-body visibility
   - elbows straight
   - shoulders above hands
   - stable hand position
   - compression rhythm 100-120/min in practice mode
   - full recoil reminder

2. Dataset collection:
   - certified instructors performing correct technique
   - learners performing common errors
   - varied phones, lighting, clothing, body types, camera distances
   - side-view and front-view comparison

3. Labelled feedback:
   - correct posture
   - bent elbows
   - shoulders behind hands
   - hands out of frame
   - poor rhythm
   - unsafe training setup

4. Safety wording:
   - Training only
   - Not a medical device
   - Does not certify CPR skill
   - In a real emergency, call emergency services first

5. App-store documentation:
   - privacy policy
   - camera usage explanation
   - location usage explanation
   - medical disclaimer
   - emergency-number verification process
